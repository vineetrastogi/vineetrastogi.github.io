Asking Good Questions

January 1, 2015

Asking good questions. It seems pretty straightforward, and easy to do. I thought as much when starting Phase 0, but realized quickly, that there is a lot of importance in how the question is crafted, the audience whom the question is directed towards, the context of the question, and many other factors. Ever since I was a kid, I've been told my teachers and professors that "there are no dumb questions". While this encourages people to ask more questions, I now feel that this can be misleading.

In my opinion, a good question needs to be specific and contextually sound. Questions, especially related to programming, need to give enough context and be focused enough for the reader to be able to answer effectively. I have made more of an effort to ask questions which help me understand a concept better, so that I won't have to ask five or ten more later on.

Questions which I have struggled with getting answered have been ones that are open to interpretation and not based on enough factual information. Bad questions that I have asked have required me to do extra research, be re-structured, and don't allow for a concise answer.

Overall, before Phase 0, I never really analyzed the questions I asked, and I didn't think that they needed to be carefully crafted. It has been a great experience succeeding and failing at asking good questions, and It is always exciting to receive useful advice as a result.

Thanks for reading,
