Issues Facing the Tech Industry - Net Neutrality

November 19, 2014

Im sure you've heard, multiple times, in recent months about the concept of Net Neutrality. I wanted to use this blog post to give a brief overview of what Net Neutrality is, what it entails, and why I am supportive of it.

What is Net Neutrality?

This concept essentially says that the Internet should be "open", meaning that internet providers should not choose the content and web-sites that you are allowed to visit, or that are available to you.

Why do I believe it is the right thing to pursue?

In my opinion, the Internet should be a space where its users can promote innovation, creativity, and lawfully visit any site they want. Internet providers have become very competitive in the last decade leading to very focused and specific goals by each company. This allows for the unequal treatment of internet traffic by these providers based on the content which they deem to be most useful or valuable. An example of this may be cable companies slowing down users' access to Netflix as to promote their television services. Net neutrality lowers the barriers to entry for online entrepreneurs, and promotes the creation of new online services and entitites.

Personally, having grown up in the generation which has been so profoundly centered around technology, it is unnerving for me that companies may have a stranglehold on the Internet, and I hope that more legislation is passed to protect this thing that has become so ingrained in our every day lives.
